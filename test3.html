Tests required during the third trimester of pregnancy:

  • Group B strep (GBS) screening:

    Rechecks for GBS colonization closer to delivery.

  • Biophysical profile (BPP):

    Assesses fetal well-being through ultrasound and non-stress test.

  • Non-stress test (NST):

    Monitors fetal heart rate and movement.

  • Contraction stress test (CST):

    Evaluates fetal heart rate response to contractions.

  • Amniotic fluid index (AFI) assessment:

    Measures the amount of amniotic fluid to assess fetal well-being.

  • Cervical dilation and effacement check:

    Assesses progress toward labor.

  • Group B strep (GBS) re-screening:

    If previous screening was negative, it may be repeated later in pregnancy.

  • Rhogam injection (if Rh negative):

    May be repeated if needed to prevent Rh incompatibility complications.



Note: Please for more Information and Details consult with your doctor

Tests required during the first trimester of pregnancy:

  • Blood type and Rh factor:

    Determines your blood type and whether you have Rh factor, which can affect pregnancy.

  • Complete blood count (CBC):

    Checks for various blood disorders and provides important information about your overall health.

  • Urinalysis:

    Examines urine for signs of diabetes, urinary tract infections, and other health issues.

  • Blood sugar levels:

    Measures glucose levels to screen for gestational diabetes.

  • Rubella immunity:

    Tests for immunity to rubella (German measles) to prevent birth defects.

  • Hepatitis B screening:

    Checks for hepatitis B infection, which can be transmitted to the baby during childbirth.

  • Syphilis test:

    Detects syphilis infection, which can cause serious complications if untreated.

  • Pap smear:

    Checks for cervical cancer and abnormalities in the cervix.

  • Ultrasound:

    Uses sound waves to create an image of the baby and check for any developmental issues.



Note: Please for more Information and Details consult with your doctor

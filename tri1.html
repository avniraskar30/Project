WELCOME TO THE FIRST TRIMESTER OF YOUR PREGNANCY

First Trimester: Changes in Mother's Body

During the first trimester of pregnancy, the mother's body undergoes significant changes to support the growing fetus:

  • Hormonal Changes: Increased levels of hormones like estrogen and progesterone can cause symptoms such as nausea, fatigue, and breast tenderness.
  • Morning Sickness: Many women experience nausea and vomiting, especially in the morning, although it can occur at any time of day.
  • Increased Urination: Hormonal changes and pressure on the bladder can lead to more frequent urination.
  • Changes in Breast Size: Breasts may become tender, swollen, or more sensitive as they prepare for breastfeeding.
  • Food Cravings and Aversions: Some women develop cravings for certain foods, while others may experience aversions to foods they once enjoyed.
  • Fatigue: Increased levels of progesterone can cause feelings of exhaustion, especially during the first trimester.

It's important for expectant mothers to listen to their bodies, get plenty of rest, and eat a balanced diet to support their health and the health of their growing baby.

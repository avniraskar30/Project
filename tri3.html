WELCOME TO THE THIRD TRIMESTER OF YOUR PREGNANCY

Third Trimester: Changes in Mother's Body

During the third trimester of pregnancy, the mother's body experiences significant changes in preparation for childbirth:

  • Increased Fetal Movement: The baby's movements may become more pronounced and frequent as it grows larger and has less room to move.
  • Braxton Hicks Contractions: Women may experience practice contractions, also known as Braxton Hicks contractions, which help prepare the uterus for labor.
  • Shortness of Breath: As the uterus expands, it can push against the diaphragm and lungs, causing shortness of breath.
  • Pelvic Pressure and Discomfort: The baby's descent into the pelvis can cause pressure and discomfort in the pelvic area.
  • Increased Urination: Pressure on the bladder from the growing uterus may lead to more frequent urination.
  • Swelling: Some women may experience swelling, particularly in the hands, feet, and ankles, due to increased fluid retention.

It's important for expectant mothers to continue with regular prenatal care, monitor fetal movements, and prepare for labor and childbirth during the third trimester.
